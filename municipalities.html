MUNICIPALITIES
THE TEN TOWN OF MOUNTAIN PROVINCE
1.Tadian - " Gateway to the West"

Tadian is the gateway to the west with its connection to a national road to its western neighboring province, Ilocos Sur. In the early days, Tadian was just a barrio. The seat of gvernment was then situated in Kayan. In 1959, by virtue of RA 2101, the "municipyo" was transferred to Tadian. In June 25, 1963, Tadian became a full-fledged and regular municpality by operation of EO No. 42.

2.Bauko - "The Vegetable Bowl of Mt. Province"

Bauko welcomes its people and visitors who enter Mt. Provnce from the City of Baguio and Benguet Province via the Halsema Highway with a nice welcome arch at Mount Data. Bauko has its boundaries with Benguet Province and Ifugao Province in the south, Sagada in the north, Sabangan in the east, and Tadian in the west. Bauko is the most populated town in Mt. Province composed of 22 barangays which are sub-grouped into the upper area and lower area. There is a campus of the Mt. Province State University in Bauko, while the Louis Hora Memorial Regional Hospital is located at barrio Abatan.

3.Sabangan

Sabangan is in the western part of Mountaen Province with its boundaries on the north by Sagada and Bontoc, on the east by Ifugao Province, and on the south and west by Bauko. With 15 barangays, Sabangan is mainly agricultural with rice, camote, cassava, gabi, vegetables, fruits, and coffee being the main crops. A first industrial project in Sabangan is a mini-hydro electric plant currently being built in Barangay Napua along the Chico River. Sabangan is also known as loom weaving town specializing in placemats, napkins, table runners, and bags.

4.Sagada - "Paradise in the Mountains"

Sitting on the upper end of the Malitep tributary of the Chico River, Sagada is located between the Cordillera Mountains, the Ilocos ranges of Bessang and Tirad, and Mt. Sipsipitan in Abra. Sagada was once an "ili" first settled by a man named Biag who fled his village of Bika, Abra from marauding headhunters. Biag first went to Candon, Ilocos Sur but when baptism was enforced by the Spaniards thereat, he moved back to the mountains until he settled in Sagada. It is not known whether Biag had a name for his settlement, now known as Sagada, but accounts of a story tell of two exploring Spanish soldiers who met a man carrying a bamboo basket used to catch fish, and asked the man the name of the place. Thinking that the soldiers were asking what he was carrying, the man answered "sagada".

5.Besao - "The Town with a Stone Calendar"

Besao lies on the western most part of Mt. Province; bounded on the north by Tubo, Abra, on the south by Tadian, on the east by Sagada, and on the west by Quirino, Ilocos Sur. While the poetic name of Besao is Golinsan, it is believed that it derived its name from the Ilocano word "busol" or "buso" because the "Besaos"were fierce tribal warriors during the primitive times protecting and defending their land from invaders. "Besaos" later evolved to Besao, and thereafter the town carried this name up to the present. A peaceful town of some 8,000 people, the people of Besao belongs to the Aplai tribe majority of whom are Christians belonging to the Philippine Episcopal Church (Anglican).

6.Bontoc - "The Capital Town"

Bontoc was the capital town of the Mountain Province, which comprised before 1966, the sub-provinces of Benguet, Ifugao, Bontoc, Apayao, and Kalinga. Today, Bontoc remains as the capital and set of government of the new and now Mt. Province. Bontoc is the center and hub of business, education, health, and even religion of the province. The Mt. Province State University and the Bontoc General Hospital have their main campuses in Bontoc.

7.Sadanga - "Home of Indigenous Living Tradition"

Sadanga is 25 kilometers northeast of Bontoc, and has its boundaries in the north by Kalinga Province. Home of living tradition, Sadanga was created by EO No. 235 issued by President Ramon Magsaysay in 1956. In June 25, 1963, Sadanga became a regular and independent municipality by virtue of another EO No. 42 signed by President Diosdado Macapagal. Sadanga has only 8 barangays. The people depend on rice growing, hunting and construction work for institutions and individuals for their livelihood and income.

8.Barlig - "A Shangri-La Town"

Barlig is a Shangri-La town with the towering mountains specifically Mt. Amuyao, the 3rd hghest mountain in the Philippines at 2,862 meters above sea level. The mountain hosts thick and mossy forests with varieties of orchids, forest plants and trees that provide food and shelter to the many species of birds and wild animals, including the elusive Philippine eagle.

9.Natonin - "Land of Tranquiility"

Natonin is a land of tranquility nestled serenely between the majestic peaks of Mt. Amuyao, Mt. Oyayao, and Mt. Tinufuan. The thick rainforests account for the fact that Natonin is literally blessed with splashes from the numerous waterfalls dropping from the mountain sides that form streams and rivers. Natonin hosts four ethno-linguistic tribes of unsure origins; i-Farangaos, i-Majukayong, i-Kachakran, and i-Balangao. It is amazing that these tribes would understand each of the other when they converse and communicate.

10.Paracelis - "A Future Trading Hub and Growth Center"

Paracelis, located in the easternmost part of Mt. Province, was originally a barrio belonging to the Municipality of Natonin. By enactment of Republic Act 3488, it was separated on June 16, 1962 from Natonin to become a full-fledged municipality and was named Paracale. There are 9 barangays in Paracelis with 37% of its population living the urban locations on a basin of low-level to hilly and flat lands in the western part of the town while the remainder of 67% are in the rural areas.
